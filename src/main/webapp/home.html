Kathmandu, March 2 The government has decided not to issue on-arrival visas for the citizens of five countries which have been the worst hit by the novel coronavirus outbreak. The countries include China, Japan, South Korea, Iran, and Italy. The decision will come into effect from this Friday. Initially, the decision will be implemented till the end of March. The citizens from 12 countries affected by civil wars and other terrorist activities had been barred from this facility. Ishwar Raj Paudel, the chief at the Department of Immigration, says the citizens of the additional five countries now need to obtain a visa from the Nepali missions in the concerned countries if they wish to visit Nepal.
